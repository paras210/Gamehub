Game Zone

Tic Tac Toe

Tic Tac Toe is a classic board game where two players take turns marking Xs and Os on a grid, aiming to form a line of three of their symbols to win.

Dot and Boxes

Dot and Boxes is a game where two players take turns connecting dots to form lines, aiming to create boxes.

Connect 4

Connect 4 is a game where the objective is to be the first to align four of your colored discs on a grid.

Guess The Number

Guess the Number is a game in which one player thinks of a number and another player tries to guess it.

Rock Paper Scissor

Rock, Paper, Scissors is a popular hand game where two players choose from three simple hand signs: Rock, Paper, or Scissors.

Hangman

Hangman is a word game where a player thinks of a word and another player tries to guess it by suggesting letters one at a time.

Tic Tac Toe

  • Game Grid: Tic-Tac-Toe is played on a 3x3 grid, creating nine cells for gameplay.
  • Two Players: The game is typically played by two players, one using "X" and the other using "O" as their symbols.
  • Turn-Based: Players take turns to make a move, starting with one player (often "X") and then alternating.
  • Winning Combinations: A player wins by placing three of their symbols consecutively in a row, column, or diagonal. For example, if "X" occupies the top row horizontally, "X" wins the game.
  • Strategy and Tactics: Tic-Tac-Toe involves strategy and tactics to block the opponent's moves while trying to create your winning combination. It's a game of skill and planning.

Rock Paper Scissor

  • Two Players:The game is played between two players.
  • Three Shapes: Each player simultaneously selects one of three shapes: Rock, Paper, or Scissors.
  • Winning Combinations:
    • Rock beats Scissors.
    • Scissors beats Paper.
    • Paper beats Rock.

Connect 4

  • Game Board: Connect Four is played on a vertically standing grid with 6 rows and 7 columns, creating 42 slots in total.
  • Two Players: The game is typically played by two players, one using red discs and the other using yellow discs.
  • Objective: The objective is to be the first player to connect four of their discs consecutively in a row, column, or diagonal.
  • Turn-Based:Players take turns to drop one of their discs into any of the empty slots at the top of a column. The disc falls to the lowest available slot in that column.
  • Winning Combinations: A player wins by forming a line of four of their discs, either horizontally, vertically, or diagonally. The line can be either ascending or descending.

Dot and Boxes

  • Game Grid: Dot and Boxes is played on a grid of dots, typically a square grid, with multiple rows and columns. The size of the grid can vary.
  • Two Players: The game is typically played by two players, although it can be played by more in variations. Players take turns.
  • Objective: The objective is to create as many boxes as possible by drawing horizontal and vertical lines between adjacent dots.
  • Winning: The game continues until no more lines can be drawn. The player who creates the most boxes by the end of the game wins. The player with the most boxes is determined by counting the initials inside each box.

Hangman

  • Word Selection: One player selects a word and keeps it secret from the other player(s). The word should be chosen from a specific category (e.g., animals, movies) and can include spaces and punctuation.
  • Word Display:For the other player(s), the word is displayed as a series of underscores, one for each letter in the word. For example, "apple" is displayed as "_ _ _ _ _."
  • Guessing Letters: Players take turns guessing letters one at a time. They can guess any letter from the alphabet.
  • Correct Guesses:If a player guesses a letter that is in the word, the letter is revealed in its correct position(s). For example, if the word is "apple" and a player guesses "p," the display becomes "_ _ _ p p _."
  • Incorrect Guesses:If a player guesses a letter that is not in the word, a part of the "hangman" (typically a gallows with a noose) is drawn. The number of incorrect guesses allowed before the hangman is fully drawn varies but is usually around six incorrect guesses.
  • Winning:The guessing player(s) win if they successfully guess all the letters in the word before the hangman is fully drawn. They lose if the hangman is drawn completely without guessing the word.

Guess the Number

  • Objective: The objective of the game is to guess the correct number chosen by the computer or another player.
  • Players: This game can be played by one player (against the computer) or two or more players taking turns to guess.
  • Guessing: Players make guesses by selecting a number from the established range. They input their guess, and it's checked against the secret number.
  • Winning:The game continues until a player correctly guesses the secret number. When that happens, they are declared the winner. In a multiplayer game, the player who guesses correctly first wins. In a single-player game, the player aims to beat their own best time or score.
